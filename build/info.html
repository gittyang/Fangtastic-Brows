FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is microblading?

Microblading is considered a semi-permanent eyebrow tattoo that can dramatically correct or fully reconstruct lost or thinning brows due to aging or over tweezed brows. It is also an excellent choice for those wanting to simply enchance their brows to create a fuller and more enriched look. A microbladed tattoo typically lasts 1-3 years, depending on skin type and skincare routine. The procedure is done by using a hand tool with ultra fine needles (blades) and each 'hair' is gently etched into the skin to create a natural and realistic look. Choice of pigment/coloring are chosen to match the natural or desired brow color as well as the person's skin tone.

Who is not suitable for the procedure?

Microblading is not recommended if you meet any of the following conditions:

  • Using Accutane.

  • Pregnant or nursing.

  • Have a heart condition that requires medication and medical device(s).

  • Have diabetes and other blood disorders such as hemophilia.

  • Have skin diseases (psoriasis, excema, etc.), or a skin infection (rash, blisters, etc.)

  • Allergic to makeup or topical anesthetics.

  • Sick with a cold or flu-virus.

What is the microblading process?

Microblading is a 2-step process and takes at least two appointments to complete. The first appointment will consist of the initial assessment of the client's brows followed by the first microblading session. After a 6-8 week healing period, the second appointment (also known as the perfection session) can then be arranged to provide any necessary touch up on the client's brows.

What is the pre-care procedure?

  • NO alcohol or caffeine 48 hours prior to your appointment.

  • Do not take Aspirin, Advil/Ibuprofen, Niacin, or any blood-thinning vitamins 72 hours prior to your appointment.

  • Avoid sun-burn and tanning one week prior to your appointment so that we can accurately assess your skin tone and pigment color selection.

  • No face scrubs or chemical peels at least two weeks prior.

  • You're welcome to bring your personal brow makeup with you to your appointment so that the technician can get a better picture of your everyday look.
    If you have picture references to the shapes and colors of your ideal brow style, that would be helpful too.

What should I expect during my first session? (1.5 - 2 hours)

During the first microblading session, the technician will shape and style the client's brows to their satisfaction. This includes how high of an arc the client wants, length of brow, thickness, etc. Next will be the pigment color selection in which the technician and the client determines what color best matches the their natural or desired brow color.

Once the client is satisfied with the choices, we begin the microblading procedure. First the technician will clean the brow area and apply a topical anesthetic to numb the area. After a few minutes, the technician will begin the microblading process. Additional anesthetic may be reapplied if necessary.

What should I do during my 6-8 weeks healing period

After your first session, the eyebrow area will be slightly red and the color of your eyebrow will appear very dark. This is normal for the procedure as the new pigments have just been etched on the skin and your eyebrows are in the healing process. In the following days, the eyebrows will continue to be dark and you will notice slight scabbing. Do not pick at the scabs as this may damage the skin and the outcome of the microbladed area.

The eyebrows will lighten up in color as the overall color will fade by up to 40% as it continues to heal. It will take approximately 7-14 days for the brows to heal over and about 1 month for the color to fully set in. You'll notice the color is very light right after the two weeks of healing; the skin will have an opaque milky looking quality to it. This is due to the tissue still healing from the inside out even though the surface of the skin is healed. The true color will reveal itself once healed.

In the mean time, it's recommended that you continue to follow post-care procedures to ensure the best outcome for your brows.

What's the second microblading session for? (1.5 - 2 hours)

After your eyebrows has had the chance to heal for a period of 6 - 8 weeks, a touch up is necessary to address any other minor details in your eyebrows as everyone heals differently and the outcome will vary. For example, certain areas may not have been touched on during the first session and may need to be refilled, pigment color may need to be adjusted, or brow thickness and more strokes may need to be added.

Clients with oilier skin often require a deeper shade during the touch up session as the oils tend to lighten the pigment during the healing process, so this session gives the technician an opportunity to address that. Essentially, your second touch up session is when we refine and detail your brows to ensure that it looks the way you want it to so that you can wake up every morning with perfect brows!

What is the post-care procedure?

  • Follow aftercare instructions provided by your technician.

  • Keep the brow area clean by gently rinsing with water and avoid the use of abrasive washcloths or sponges.

  • Allow eyebrows to completely air dry before applying healing ointment(Vaseline, Aquaphor, etc.)

  • Do not rub, pick, or scratch the healing area. Let any scabbing or dry skin naturally exfoliate off.

  • No makeup or cosmetic products to be used on the healing area for two weeks, using makeup may cause an infection.

  • Avoid sun exposure and tanning for minimum of 4 weeks after your procedure. Direct sunlight can cause the pigment to change color while it’s healing.

  • Avoid sweating for 2 weeks. Sweating may expel pigment through the skin producing poor results.
